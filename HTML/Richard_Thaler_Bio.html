Introduction

Richard Thaler is an American Economist and a professer at the Unuversity of Chicago's Booth School of Business. Thaler is one of the pioners in behavioral finance. He was a frequent collaborator with Amos Tversky and Daniel Kanhneman. In 2017, Thaler was awarded the Nobel Prize in Economics.

Books

Thaler is the author of a number of books including such works as Quasi-Rational Economics and The Winners Curse. He also co-authored Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness with Cass Sunstein. Nudge discusses how public and private organizations can help people make better choices in their daily lives. More recently, Thaler wrote Misbehaving: The Making of Behavioral Economics which is part-memoir and part general history of the devolopment of bahvioral economics and the conflicts with the reining economic orthodoxy of the time.
